## Overview

Experiment 003 executed the full investigative pipeline on *The Great Gatsby* within a Mandarin Chinese cultural and intellectual context. The investigation was conducted explicitly from a Chinese intellectual tradition that emphasizes relationships, continuous adaptation, and the dynamic tension between person and reality — rather than the individualist, epistemic, or historical frames that shaped Experiments 001 and 002.

The essay describes its own methodology explicitly, a feature not present in the English or Spanish executions:

> **观察 → 分类 → 问题生成 → 收集证据 → 修正假设 → 再次验证**  
> *(Observation → Classification → Question Generation → Evidence Collection → Hypothesis Revision → Re-verification)*

This is not incidental. The Mandarin investigator independently derived the same cognitive sequence that the Hermeneia architecture implements. See Section 7 below.

---

## 1. Governing Question

**一个人是否能够拒绝现实的变化，而仍然要求现实按照自己的愿望继续存在？**

*Can a person refuse the changes of reality and still demand that reality continue according to their own wishes?*

This question is neither the English question ("Can an interpretation become more real to someone than reality itself?" — epistemic) nor the Spanish question ("Can a person ever separate themselves from their history?" — historical-relational). It is **processual**: it asks whether a person can refuse the *ongoing process* of adapting one's understanding to changing reality.

The governing frame is: **现实—关系—变化—调整** (Reality—Relationship—Change—Continuous Adaptation).

## 3. Classification Stability

The Mandarin investigator explicitly distinguishes three device categories:

- **隐喻 (Metaphor):** Participates in the novel's entire meaning system; develops across the novel; not merely local
- **象征 (Symbol):** Has significant meaning but primarily serves a local role; does not drive conceptual development across the whole novel
- **叙事手法 (Narrative Device):** Affects reader emotion and narrative rhythm but does not constitute core metaphor

This classification system maps directly onto the categories used in the English and Spanish executions (metaphor, symbol, narrative device). The same textual elements received the same classifications.

**Finding:** Classification stability holds for the third consecutive execution.

## 4. Question Divergence

Three executions, three distinct governing questions:

| Experiment | Language | Governing Question | Frame |
|---|---|---|---|
| 001 | English | Can an interpretation become more real than reality itself? | Epistemic |
| 002 | Spanish | Can a person ever separate themselves from their history? | Historical-relational |
| 003 | Mandarin | Can a person refuse reality's changes and still demand reality conform to their wishes? | Processual-adaptive |

None of these questions appears in Fitzgerald's text. All three are grounded in the text's evidence. All three are non-contradictory — they are different investigative paths into the same body of evidence.

**Finding:** Question divergence holds for the third consecutive execution. The governing question is the primary point of differentiation between executions from the same corpus.

## 5. Evidence Weighting

The Mandarin governing question produced a distinctive evidence weighting profile:

**High weight in Mandarin:**
- Reality preceding desire (现实先于欲望) — established narratively; Gatsby enters a world already in motion
- Gatsby's refusal to update his *cognitive model* (认知框架) of Daisy versus her actual present reality
- The Plaza Hotel confrontation as the moment Gatsby demands reality deny what has already happened
- Nick's continuous revision of his own understanding — the adaptive contrast to Gatsby
- Wilson's catastrophic inability to adapt — unable to make incremental adjustments; system collapse

**Lower weight in Mandarin (prominent in other executions):**
- Self-invention, reinvention of identity (dominant in English)
- Family memory, continuity of blood and belonging (dominant in Spanish)
- The nostalgia of the past as a living force pulling backward (Spanish emphasis)

**Finding:** Evidence weighting follows from the governing question, consistent with the pattern found in Experiments 001 and 002.

## 6. The Five-Character Typology

The Mandarin execution produced an analysis structure not present in the English or Spanish executions: a complete five-character typology organized by *mode of response to change*.

| Character | Mode of Response to Change |
|---|---|
| Gatsby (盖茨比) | Fixed understanding — refuses to update internal cognitive model |
| Nick (尼克) | Continuous revision — updates his understanding as evidence accumulates |
| Daisy (黛西) | Relational stability maintenance — adjusts behavior to preserve relationships |
| Tom (汤姆) | Structural dependence — relies on existing power position; no need to adapt |
| Wilson (威尔逊) | Catastrophic collapse — unable to make incremental adjustments; breaks entirely |

This typology is a consequence of the governing question. Once the question is "how do people respond to changing reality?", every character becomes an instance in a response taxonomy.

Nick's role is more prominent in the Mandarin execution than in either the English or Spanish. The essay frames Nick explicitly as the *exemplar of the correct relationship with reality* — continuous, adaptive, non-controlling. This emphasis reflects a Chinese intellectual tradition that values this adaptive pattern.

## 7. The Meta-Observation: The Essay Describes Hermeneia

The Mandarin essay's methodology section (Section 二) explicitly describes its analytical procedure:

> **观察 → 分类 → 问题生成 → 收集证据 → 修正假设 → 再次验证**

And it articulates the epistemological principles governing that procedure:

> "文本先于解释，证据先于理论，修正先于结论。"  
> *Text precedes interpretation, evidence precedes theory, revision precedes conclusion.*

This is the Hermeneia pipeline, derived independently from within the investigation itself.

The methodology section also notes that the investigator initially hypothesized the governing concept was "harmony" (和谐) — and revised that hypothesis when it could not simultaneously explain the differences between Gatsby, Nick, and Wilson. The revision yielded the actual governing concept: **持续调整 (Continuous Adaptation)**.

The essay thus demonstrates, within itself, the cognitive cycle that Hermeneia implements as an architecture: observation is separated from classification, classification from question generation, question generation from evidence gathering, evidence from interpretation, interpretation from revision. The architecture was not imposed on the inquiry. The inquiry described the architecture.

## 8. Compression

**Central compression (Mandarin):**

> 人的成熟，不在于终于控制了现实，而在于学会在不断变化的现实中持续调整自己的理解、关系与行动。  
> *Human maturity lies not in finally controlling reality, but in learning to continuously adjust one's understanding, relationships, and actions within ever-changing reality.*

**Comparison:**

| Experiment | Language | Compression |
|---|---|---|
| 001 | English | "Reality corrects interpretation." |
| 002 | Spanish | "History remains active." |
| 003 | Mandarin | "Maturity is continuous adaptation, not control." |

The three compressions do not contradict each other. They are non-contradictory summaries of the same body of evidence under three different governing questions:

- The English compression names the relationship between mind and reality (interpretations get corrected)
- The Spanish compression names the temporality of evidence (history doesn't stay past)
- The Mandarin compression names the capacity required in response (continuous adaptation)

Together they compose a richer picture than any single execution could produce.

---

## 9. Generalization Pattern

Like the Spanish execution (and unlike the English), the Mandarin execution generalized from Gatsby's individual tragedy to a broader human condition by the conclusion:

> *This understanding is not only about Gatsby's tragedy, but about the general human condition of how to adjust oneself within constantly changing reality.*

The boats metaphor receives its most expansive reading here:

> "真正的航行，不是征服水流，而是在不断变化的水流中不断调整自己的方向。"  
> *True navigation is not conquering the current, but continuously adjusting one's direction in response to the changing current.*

In the English execution, the boats are about individual fate. In the Mandarin, they become a principle of human existence.

## 11. Evidential Status Update

These findings, taken together with Experiments 001 and 002, upgrade the following from **Empirical Finding** to **Replicated Finding** (3 executions, 3 culturally distinct languages):

1. **Observation stability across cultural contexts** — the same major textual phenomena are identified regardless of the investigator's cultural frame
2. **Classification stability across cultural contexts** — the same literary device categories are applied to the same phenomena
3. **Question divergence as the primary differentiation variable** — governing questions consistently diverge across cultural contexts while observations remain stable
4. **Evidence weighting as downstream from governing question** — the governing question determines which observations receive higher salience

These findings now satisfy the experiment matrix's threshold for **Replicated Finding**: observed consistently across 3+ experiments in the same axis.
