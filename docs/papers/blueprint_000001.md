# Blueprint 000001

**Artifact:** Hermeneia White Paper  
**Ratification Status:** ✅ Ratified  
**Date:** 2026-06-25

---

## Governing Question

How can the evolution of understanding become an accountable object of study in AI-assisted inquiry?

---

## Thesis

Hermeneia is a constitutional methodology for the disciplined evolution of understanding through the separation and governance of cognitive responsibilities.

---

## Purpose

Introduce a research methodology.

Not software. Not a product. Not a framework.

A methodology for studying the evolution of understanding rather than merely generating language.

---

## Audience

**Primary:** AI researchers, digital humanities scholars, philosophers of science, educators, knowledge management practitioners.

**Secondary:** Engineers, historians, legal scholars, theologians — anyone whose work requires accountable inquiry from evidence.

---

## Required Claims

The paper must establish all five of the following. None may be omitted. None may be overstated.

1. Contemporary AI collapses multiple distinct cognitive responsibilities into a single generative act, making failures undiagnosable.
2. Separating those responsibilities makes inquiry more accountable — failures become locatable; understanding becomes traceable.
3. Constitutional governance, applied to inquiry, preserves the conditions under which understanding can mature honestly.
4. Calibration experiments produced observable, replicated findings about how investigators diverge from the same evidence.
5. Those findings justify a broader empirical research program. They do not prove its conclusion.

---

## Forbidden Claims

The paper must never imply any of the following. If a sentence implies one, it must be revised.

- Universal theory of cognition
- Universal interpretation engine
- Artificial general intelligence
- One correct meaning for any document
- Cultural determinism
- Proof of the methodology from one corpus

These are outside scope. Permanently.

---

## Calibration Artifact

The Gatsby experiments — three executions, three investigative frameworks, one corpus.

Not because Gatsby matters. Because calibration matters.

That distinction must remain explicit in the paper. The experiments calibrate the methodology. They do not validate it universally.

---

## Required Evidence for Claim 4

Claim 4 SHALL establish:

- The location of the first replicated divergence.
- The distinction between replicated observations and divergent investigations.
- That evidence weighting is downstream from governing question selection.
- A visual representation sufficient for a reviewer to reconstruct the experimental finding without relying on prose alone.

Preferred representation (implementation-independent — a future Artist may satisfy this with a figure, table, state machine, or equivalent):

```
Same Corpus
│
├── Observation          (Replicated)
├── Classification       (Replicated)
├── Governing Question   ← First Replicated Divergence
├── Evidence Weighting
├── Compression
└── Communication
```

The contract is the evidence, not the figure style. Any rendering that allows a skeptical reviewer to locate where divergence begins and trace its downstream effects satisfies this requirement.

---

## Tone

Scientific. Conservative. Evidence before assertion. Confidence proportional to evidence.

Distinguish observation from hypothesis in every section. A reader should be able to locate any claim in the paper against its evidential tier without additional context.

---

## Structure

The paper moves in one direction: from architecture to evidence to open questions.

It does not circle back to advocacy. It does not end with a sales pitch. It ends with investigation.

---

## Ending Constraint

The paper must not end with certainty.

It must end with the research program — with the question that the methodology is now positioned to answer, and an honest account of how much work remains before that answer is warranted.

The final question the reader should carry: *What if we have been measuring the outputs of understanding when we should have been measuring its evolution?*

---

## Ratification Question

Would a skeptical researcher who disagreed with Hermeneia's claims be able to:

1. State precisely what the paper claims
2. State precisely what it does not claim
3. Identify which findings are observed, which are replicated, and which are hypotheses
4. Know exactly what evidence would change the conclusions

If yes — the paper is complete.

If no — the paper needs revision.
