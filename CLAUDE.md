## What the Freeze Accomplished

The freeze prevented architectural churn. More valuably, it forced discovery of the architecture rather than continuous redesign of it.

The ideas that emerged during the freeze — Intent Hypothesis, Interpretive Exploration, Execution Contract, the evolution of understanding as the research object, the evidence-guided protocol, and the distinction between investigative frameworks and languages — did not come from adding new tables or pipeline stages. They emerged from repeatedly exercising the existing architecture against real investigations.

That is the signal that the architecture is ready. Not perfection. Demonstrated stability under use.
